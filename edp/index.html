Training Modules

12 comprehensive modules across 3 learning tracks

Track 1: AI & Technology in Education

4 modules • Hands-on labs included

Module 1

AI-Powered Classroom Tools

Use ChatGPT, Copilot, and custom GPTs to create lesson plans, auto-grade assignments, generate practice problems, and provide personalized student feedback at scale.

Module 2

AI-Based Assessment Design

Design rubrics, auto-evaluate subjective answers, detect plagiarism using AI tools, and create adaptive assessments that adjust difficulty based on student performance.

Module 3

Learning Analytics & Data-Driven Teaching

Track student progress using dashboards, identify at-risk learners early, and use data to optimize curriculum pacing and teaching effectiveness.

Module 4

Open-Source & Cloud Tools for Labs

Set up GitHub Classroom, Google Colab notebooks, and cloud-based lab environments. Teach students using the same tools used in industry.

Track 2: Modern Pedagogy & Curriculum Design

4 modules • Workshop + project-based

Module 5

Outcome-Based Education (OBE)

Design course outcomes aligned with Bloom's taxonomy, map Program Outcomes (POs) to Course Outcomes (COs), and build assessment matrices compliant with NBA/NAAC standards.

Module 6

Project-Based Learning (PBL)

Structure semester-long projects that combine theory with practice. Design milestones, evaluation rubrics, and industry-relevant problem statements for CS students.

Module 7

Industry-Aligned Syllabus Mapping

Audit existing syllabi against current industry job descriptions. Update course content to include modern tech stacks, tools, and frameworks that employers actually demand.

Module 8

Flipped Classroom & Blended Learning

Design pre-class video content, in-class active learning activities, and post-class assessments. Create an effective blended learning strategy that maximizes student engagement.

Track 3: Professional Growth & Research

4 modules • Mentorship included

Module 9

Research Paper Writing & Publication

Write research papers following IEEE/ACM standards. Understand peer review processes, journal selection, and strategies for getting published in indexed conferences.

Module 10

Student Mentorship & Career Guidance

Learn effective student mentorship techniques, career counselling frameworks, and how to guide students towards internships, higher studies, and industry placements.

Module 11

Soft Skills & Presentation Mastery

Improve classroom delivery, build engaging slide decks, handle Q&A sessions confidently, and develop storytelling techniques that keep students attentive.

Module 12

Industry Connect & Guest Lecture Setup

Build connections with industry professionals, organize guest lecture series, and create industry-academia collaborative projects for your department.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the Educator Development Program

EDP is open to all faculty members from partner universities and institutions associated with IIAT. Both junior and senior educators are welcome to apply. HODs, lab instructors, and teaching assistants are also encouraged to participate.

The EDP is structured as a 6–8 week program with flexible scheduling. Each track has 4 modules with self-paced content and live weekend workshops. You can progress at your own pace while teaching.

EDP is fully sponsored by IIAT through STTAR for faculty at partner institutions. There is no registration fee, no material cost, and no certification fee for eligible educators.

Participants receive a STTAR-certified Educator Credential upon successful completion, which is recognised across the IIAT partner university network and adds to your professional development portfolio.

Absolutely. EDP is specifically designed for working faculty. Self-paced modules can be completed at your convenience, and live sessions are scheduled on weekends. Most participants complete the program alongside their regular duties.

Institutions can partner with IIAT by contacting us at info@iiath.com or calling +91 868 866 4337. We offer customized EDP batches for partner colleges with a minimum of 10 faculty members per batch.
