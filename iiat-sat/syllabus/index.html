IIAT SAT Syllabus

Complete syllabus for the IIAT Scholarship Assessment Test. Know what to study, how much each topic weighs, and where to focus your preparation.

4
Subjects
200

Duration
Algebra High Weightage
  • Quadratic equations and expressions
  • Polynomials — factor theorem, remainder theorem
  • Sequences and series (AP, GP, HP)
  • Logarithms and exponential functions
  • Matrices and determinants (2×2, 3×3)
  • Complex numbers — basics and modulus
Calculus High Weightage
  • Limits and continuity
  • Differentiation — chain rule, product rule, quotient rule
  • Applications of derivatives (maxima, minima, tangent)
  • Basic integration — substitution and by parts
  • Definite integrals and area under curves
Coordinate Geometry Medium Weightage
  • Straight lines — slope, intercepts, angle between lines
  • Circles — equation, tangent, and normal
  • Conic sections — parabola, ellipse, hyperbola (basics)
Probability & Statistics Medium Weightage
  • Permutations and combinations
  • Probability — independent events, Bayes' theorem basics
  • Mean, median, mode, standard deviation
Trigonometry Low Weightage
  • Trigonometric identities and equations
  • Inverse trigonometric functions
  • Heights and distances
Preparation Tip

Focus heavily on Algebra and Calculus — they make up ~60% of the math section. Practice NCERT 11th & 12th chapters first, then move to competitive-level problems from Cengage or RD Sharma.

Mechanics High Weightage
  • Kinematics — motion in 1D and 2D, projectile motion
  • Newton's laws of motion and friction
  • Work, energy, and power
  • Rotational motion — moment of inertia, torque
  • Gravitation — Kepler's laws, orbital velocity
Electromagnetism High Weightage
  • Electrostatics — Coulomb's law, electric field and potential
  • Capacitors — series, parallel, energy stored
  • Current electricity — Ohm's law, Kirchhoff's laws, Wheatstone bridge
  • Magnetic effects of current — Biot-Savart, Ampere's law
  • Electromagnetic induction — Faraday's and Lenz's law
Optics & Modern Physics Medium Weightage
  • Ray optics — mirrors, lenses, prism
  • Wave optics — Young's double slit, diffraction
  • Photoelectric effect and dual nature of matter
  • Atomic structure — Bohr model, hydrogen spectrum
  • Nuclear physics — radioactivity, mass-energy equivalence
Thermodynamics & Waves Low Weightage
  • Laws of thermodynamics, heat engines, entropy
  • Wave motion — superposition, standing waves, Doppler effect
  • Fluid mechanics — Bernoulli's principle, viscosity
Preparation Tip

Mechanics and Electromagnetism are must-master sections. Start with HC Verma (Concepts of Physics) and practice numerical problems. For Modern Physics, focus on photoelectric effect and Bohr model — they appear frequently.

Analytical Reasoning High Weightage
  • Number series and pattern recognition
  • Letter and alphanumeric series
  • Coding-decoding (letter shift, symbol-based)
  • Analogies and classification
  • Blood relations
Logical Deduction High Weightage
  • Syllogisms — Venn diagram approach
  • Logical statements and conclusions
  • Cause and effect reasoning
  • Assumptions and inferences
  • Puzzles — seating arrangement, linear ordering
Data Interpretation Medium Weightage
  • Reading bar graphs, pie charts, line graphs
  • Table-based data analysis
  • Data sufficiency
Non-Verbal Reasoning Low Weightage
  • Figure series and pattern completion
  • Mirror and water images
  • Clock and calendar problems
  • Direction sense
Preparation Tip

Practice is king here. Solve 10-15 reasoning puzzles daily from RS Aggarwal (Verbal & Non-Verbal Reasoning). Focus on speed — the logic section rewards quick pattern recognition, not deep calculation.

Physical Chemistry High Weightage
  • Atomic structure and electronic configuration
  • Chemical bonding — ionic, covalent, metallic
  • States of matter — gas laws, kinetic theory
  • Chemical equilibrium and Le Chatelier's principle
  • Acids, bases, and pH scale
Inorganic Chemistry Medium Weightage
  • Periodic table — trends, groups, and blocks
  • Chemical reactions — types, balancing equations
  • Metals and non-metals — properties and reactivity series
  • Coordination compounds — basic concepts
Organic Chemistry Medium Weightage
  • Hydrocarbons — alkanes, alkenes, alkynes
  • Functional groups and IUPAC nomenclature
  • Isomerism — structural and stereoisomerism
  • Basic organic reactions — addition, substitution, elimination
Environmental & Applied Chemistry Low Weightage
  • Electrochemistry — electrolysis, galvanic cells
  • Thermochemistry — enthalpy, exo/endothermic reactions
  • Everyday chemistry — soaps, detergents, polymers
Preparation Tip

Chemistry rewards understanding over memorization. Focus on NCERT Chemistry (Class 11 & 12) for solid fundamentals. Practice balancing equations and numerical problems from Physical Chemistry. For Organic, draw structures by hand — visual learning helps with nomenclature and reaction mechanisms.
